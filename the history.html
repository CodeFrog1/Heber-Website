Heber's History

Conlfict with the natives

Before the settlers from Briham Young came to settle Heber Valey it was first occupied by the Timpanogos Utes.
When these Morman settlers built settlement in the valley the Ute tribe nearest to them started getting into conflict
with them because of the hunting grounds they took from them. The settlers built a fort inorder to protect against ute
tribe attacks. The was called the Black Hau=wk war and it went on for some time. In 1867 Chief Tabby and his whole
band came to Heber City for a peace feast. Large tables were set up in the bowery, and townswomen made a “good picnic”
for Tabby and his people. An ox was roasted barbecue style, and everyone filled up on food. The Indians stayed a few days
and then went home with presents of food. This picnic created good will, and there were few raids in Wasatch County afterwards.
